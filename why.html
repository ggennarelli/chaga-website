The gap between what's allowed in American food and what's allowed everywhere else is wider than most people realize. Chaga exists to make that gap visible — in the store, before you buy.

The regulatory gap is real

The European Union has banned or restricted over 1,300 ingredients that are perfectly legal in American products. Not obscure chemicals — ingredients in products you buy every week. In your cereal. In your children's snacks. In your sports drinks.

The FDA operates on a "generally recognized as safe" (GRAS) framework that hasn't been meaningfully updated in decades. Manufacturers can self-certify ingredients as safe without independent review. Europe requires proof of safety before approval. America requires proof of harm after the fact.

Ingredients you should know about

  • Red 40, Yellow 5, Yellow 6 — Synthetic dyes requiring hyperactivity warning labels in the EU. GRAS in the US. FDA voluntary phase-out announced 2025. Banned in West Virginia.
  • Titanium Dioxide (E171) — Banned as a food additive in the EU since 2022. Approved by the FDA. Found in candies and baked goods.
  • Potassium Bromate — Banned in the EU, UK, and Canada as a possible carcinogen. Approved by the FDA. Found in commercial breads.
  • Brominated Vegetable Oil (BVO) — Banned in the EU. FDA revoked authorization in 2024, but products containing it may persist on shelves.
  • BHA — Restricted in the EU as a possible carcinogen. GRAS in the US. Found in breakfast cereals and chips.
  • TBHQ — Restricted in the EU. GRAS in the US. Found in fast food and packaged snacks.

The pet problem nobody talks about

70% of American households have a pet. Many human foods contain ingredients that are toxic to dogs and cats — and the label won't tell you.

Xylitol is in sugar-free peanut butter, gum, and protein bars. It causes rapid insulin release, liver failure, and death in dogs. A single stick of gum can kill a small dog. There is no warning on the label.

Chaga tracks 30 pet-toxic ingredients with severity ratings from LOW to CRITICAL. When you scan a product, Chaga tells you if it's safe to have around your pets — personalized to your dog, your cat, or both.

This is not fear-mongering. These are facts documented in EU regulatory databases, FDA enforcement actions, and peer-reviewed studies. Every flag in Chaga links to its source. Verify everything we show you.
